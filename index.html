Australian Bushfires of 2020

This visualisations displays the Australian Bushfires of 2020, as recorded by satellite. It conveys both the location and magnitude (measured from the satellite in Kelvin) of such Bushfires. Bushfire information is relevant to the average Australian because they threaten health, safety, property, and the environment.

Australian Average Maximum Temperature 1975-2019

This visualisation displays the annual average of all maximum daily Temperatures, in various locations throughout Australia from 1975 to 2019. This is relevant information, as the warm temperatures displayed in this visualisation are one of the largest contributors to Australian Bushfires.

Australian Bushfire contributing conditions of 2016

This visualisations displays environmental conditions at various Australian locations. These conditions are relevant to the formation of Bushfires. Rainfall and high humidity reduces Bushfire risk by moistening vegetation. Alternatively, low humidity and high windspeeds can dry out fuels and also spread flames more rapidly, increasing the likelihood of Bushfires.
